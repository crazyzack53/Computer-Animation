Storyboard & Description

Storyboard: The animation follows a ball as it bounces across a city environment, telling a short story of momentum and chaos.

  • Frame 1: The ball falls from the top of a building.
  • Frame 2: It bounces off the ground, deforming briefly on impact.
  • Frame 3: The ball rolls from the sidewalk onto the street.
  • Frame 4: A car hits the ball, launching it into the air.
  • Frame 5: The ball smashes into a building, ricocheting off.
  • Frame 6: It flies toward a basketball hoop in a smooth arc.
  • Frame 7: The ball rolls around the rim of the hoop.
  • Frame 8: It finally drops through the hoop and lands inside.

Animation Principles Used

The animation demonstrates three Disney principles of animation.

  • 1. Squash and Stretch: Used when the ball compresses on impact to emphasize weight and elasticity.
  • 2. Anticipation: Used right before the car strikes the ball, giving a sense of buildup and impending action.
  • 3. Arc & Follow-Through: Seen when the ball rolls around the rim before dropping through the hoop.

Reflection

This project pushed me to blend realistic physics with animation principles to tell a small but complete story. The squash and stretch required fine-tuning timing to feel natural, anticipation gave the car hit impact, and arcs added flow to the basketball sequence. The hardest part was syncing all bounces smoothly across multiple surfaces while maintaining visual consistency between scenes.
