Project Overview

This final animation is a short Star Wars-inspired chase sequence built around realistic lighting and physics. The Falcon uses the asteroid field as cover while the TIE fighters pursue, creating a sequence of near-misses and a narrow escape.

Work Summary

I spent a large amount of time on this project (roughly 30–50 hours total). About half of that time went into modeling the Millennium Falcon, plus building and texturing the asteroid field. The other half was focused on lighting, physics/dynamics, and camera animation. I also spent about 3–4 hours on audio/post-processing.

This project ended up being one of the most fun assignments I’ve done in the class — I’m a big Star Wars fan, and I really enjoyed getting to bring my idea to life.

Problems Encountered & How I Solved Them

  • Texturing (Falcon looked flat / too clean): I spent a lot of time in Hypershade experimenting with nodes and combining maps until I could build a more weathered, believable look.
  • Physics issues (no collisions + gravity wouldn’t turn off): The solution was connecting Bullet properly to the MASH dynamics setup. After that, I could disable gravity and get collisions working.
  • Modeling challenges on the Falcon: Many issues came from extrusions behaving differently depending on component mode. I improved by learning when to select vertices vs edges vs faces before extruding/beveling.
  • Asteroids didn’t look realistic enough: I added a cloud/noise-style node in the material to create a bumpier surface and break up the silhouette.
